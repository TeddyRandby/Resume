Summary

  • The Hurlbert Lab at UNC Chapel Hill accumulated a flat csv file containing thousands of rows of data concerning North American birds and their diets. My team created a web-app which allows users to easily make complex queries against this database.

Seven-On

Role

  • React Frontend Developer

Responsibilities

  • Communicated with client
  • Practiced modular design
  • Designed for mobile first

Summary

  • The quality streamed coverage of Ultimate Frisbee games has gotten better in recent years, but lacked a way to help commentators track the players on the field. I created an app which let the commentators create teams and update the players on the field. The two teams would then be rendered on two different routes ( /home and /away) in order to allow a livestream to capture the screen and display the two teams live as they are updated by commentators.

Role

  • Fullstack Developer

Responsibilities

  • Built a web app from scratch
  • Built and integrated an API

Summary

  • Tournament directors of Ultimate Frisbee tournaments often have trouble getting teams to report the scores of their games in a timely manner. And for most tournments, there is no central place to go to find real-time scores (except for each team's twitter account). I sought to resolve these two problems by creating a platform where directors could create teams, assign games, and allow teams to self-report the scores of their games - all live, where anyone with a link can view the scores in real time.
